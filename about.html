About the Augustinian Federation for Africa

Unity, Spirituality, and Service Across the Continent

Our History

From humble beginnings to a continental federation

The Augustinian Federation for Africa (AFA) was formally established in 1995, following a series of meetings among Augustinian superiors across the African continent who recognized the need for greater collaboration and shared vision.

The initial meeting took place in Nairobi, Kenya, with representatives from Augustinian communities in Kenya, Tanzania, Nigeria, Ghana, and South Africa. These early visionaries saw the potential for a unified Augustinian witness in Africa.

In 2001, AFA received formal recognition from the Order of Saint Augustine (O.S.A.) in Rome, establishing it as an official continental body within the global Augustinian family.

Today, AFA represents Augustinian communities in over 20 African countries, working together to promote Augustinian values, support formation, and coordinate mission activities across the continent.

Our Mission

To promote and strengthen Augustinian life and mission in Africa through:

  • Fostering fraternal unity among Augustinian communities
  • Supporting the formation of Augustinians at all levels
  • Coordinating mission and service activities
  • Promoting Augustinian spirituality and theology
  • Advocating for justice, peace, and integral human development

Our Vision

A vibrant, united, and prophetic Augustinian presence in Africa, contributing to the transformation of society through the witness of communities living in harmony, seeking God together, and serving with love.

We envision an Africa where Augustinian values of community, interiority, and love for truth inspire individuals and societies toward greater unity, justice, and peace.

Our Core Values

Guided by the teachings of Saint Augustine of Hippo

Community

Living together in harmony, sharing all things in common, and supporting one another in our journey toward God.

Interiority

Turning inward to discover God who is closer to us than we are to ourselves, as Augustine taught.

Love for Truth

Seeking truth with humility and openness, recognizing that all truth leads to God, who is Truth itself.

Service

Following Christ who came not to be served but to serve, especially those who are poor and marginalized.

African Inculturation

Rooting Augustinian spirituality in the rich cultural and spiritual soil of Africa.

Peace & Reconciliation

Working for peace, healing divisions, and building bridges in a continent marked by conflict.
